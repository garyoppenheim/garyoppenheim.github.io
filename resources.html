Statistical analysis

  • R
    • Using BLAS for multicore support on Mac, for MUCH faster performance (YMMV)
    • RStudio (user-friendly GUI and programming environment)
      • RMarkdown (don't just write code line by line. preserve and compile it for tractable, reproducible analyses)
    • ggplot (graphics)
    • lme4 (mixed effects regressions via lmer, glmer)
      • RePsychLing (package for identifying parsimonious random effects structures)
        install.packages("devtools") devtools::install_github( "dmbates/RePsychLing")
      • lmerTest (a convenient way to get p-values for lmers)
      • Some tips for speeding up (g)lmer model fitting
      • lmer-related literature (see also the 2008 special issue of JML)
        • Baayen, R. H. (2008). Analyzing Linguistic Data: A practical introduction to statistics (could use an update, but excellent coverage of many issues)
        • Baayen & Milin (2010). Analyzing reaction times. International Journal of Psychological Research (a nice practical overview of an lmer workflow)
        • Barr et al (2013). Random effects structure for confirmatory hypothesis testing: Keep it maximal. Journal of Memory and Language (an explanation of and argument for maximal random effects structures; not sure I completely agree, but it's important to understand)
        • Bates et al (201x). Parsimonious mixed models. (a response to Barr et al's directive; see the vignettes in the github repository for workflow examples)
    • Resolving gfortran errors on MacOS
